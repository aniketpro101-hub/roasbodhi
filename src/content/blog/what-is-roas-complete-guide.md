Many business owners in India ask their agencies: "How did my ads perform this month?" The agency responds with metrics like "1 lakh impressions," "5,000 clicks," or "₹2 CPC." While these metrics sound impressive, they do not tell you the most important number: **How much revenue did those ads actually generate for your business?**

To answer this, you must understand **ROAS** (Return on Ad Spend). In this guide, we explain what ROAS is, how to calculate it, and why it is the key to measuring digital marketing success.

## 1. What is ROAS?

Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) is a marketing metric that measures the amount of revenue your business earns for every rupee spent on advertising. It is the direct measure of ad campaign efficiency.

### The Formula:
$$\text{ROAS} = \frac{\text{Total Revenue Generated from Ads}}{\text{Total Cost of Ad Spend}}$$

For example:
*   If you spend ₹10,000 on Facebook Ads in a month.
*   Those ads generate ₹40,000 in sales.
*   Your ROAS is:
    $$\frac{40,000}{10,000} = 4\text{X}$$
    This means for every ₹1 you spent on ads, you earned ₹4 in revenue.

---

## 2. Why is ROAS Important?

Unlike impressions or clicks, ROAS directly connects marketing spend to business revenue.

*   **Identifies Profitable Campaigns:** If Campaign A has a 4.5X ROAS and Campaign B has a 1.2X ROAS, you know exactly where to allocate your budget.
*   **Controls Budget Allocation:** Knowing your ROAS helps you scale your budget confidently. If you know that spending ₹1,00,000 yields ₹4,00,000 in revenue, scaling your business becomes a simple math equation.

---

## 3. What is a "Good" ROAS?

A "good" ROAS varies by industry and business profit margins.

*   **Under 2X ROAS:** Generally unprofitable. You are likely losing money after product costs, shipping, and service fees.
*   **3X ROAS:** The baseline target for local storefronts. Most service businesses break even or earn a profit at this level.
*   **4X+ ROAS:** High performance. Your campaigns are highly optimized, and you should consider scaling your ad budgets.
